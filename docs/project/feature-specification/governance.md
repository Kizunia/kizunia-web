# Governance

## Purpose

The Governance module defines how Kizunia Web is operated, moderated, and maintained.

It establishes the trust model of the platform by defining who is responsible for managing content, reviewing community contributions, assigning verification, and ensuring that information published on Kizunia remains accurate.

Unlike other modules, Governance does not directly provide features to builders.

Instead, it provides the rules and workflows that keep the platform trustworthy, reliable, and community-driven.

---

# Philosophy

Kizunia Web is community-driven but curated.

The platform should encourage community participation while ensuring that published information remains accurate and trustworthy.

Rather than restricting all management to administrators, Kizunia empowers trusted individuals to maintain different parts of the platform while preserving a clear review process.

Every contributor should have the opportunity to improve the platform.

Not every contribution should become public immediately.

---

# Governance Model

Kizunia follows a layered trust model.

```text
Community
      │
      ▼
Trusted Maintainers
      │
      ▼
Administrators
      │
      ▼
Platform Owner
```

Each level has greater responsibility rather than greater importance.

---

# Platform Owner

The Platform Owner has complete authority over Kizunia Web.

Responsibilities include:

* Platform direction
* Product decisions
* Governance
* Administrator management
* Verification management
* Global moderation
* Platform configuration

The Platform Owner is ultimately responsible for the integrity of the platform.

---

# Administrators

Administrators assist in operating the platform.

They may:

* Review community submissions
* Approve hackathons
* Verify users
* Verify projects
* Feature content
* Moderate reports
* Manage maintainers
* Review edit suggestions

Administrators should focus on maintaining quality rather than controlling content.

---

# Maintainers

Maintainers are trusted users responsible for specific entities.

Examples include:

* Project Maintainers
* Hackathon Maintainers
* Team Maintainers

Maintainers manage only the entities they have been assigned.

They do not receive platform-wide administrative privileges.

---

# Community Contributions

registered user should have the opportunity to contribute to the platform.

Examples include:

* Suggesting hackathons
* Suggesting edits
* Reporting outdated information
* Reporting incorrect links
* Improving project information

Community contributions are encouraged as a way of keeping the platform accurate and up to date.

---

# Review Queue

Community contributions should never modify published information immediately.

Instead, every contribution enters a review queue.

```text
Community Suggestion
        │
        ▼
Review Queue
        │
        ▼
Owner / Admin / Maintainer
        │
        ▼
Approved
        │
        ▼
Published
```

Rejected suggestions should never modify the public platform.

---

# Verification System

Verification exists to establish authenticity.

Verification is **not** a popularity system.

It simply answers the question:

> "Why should this person or entity be trusted?"

Verification should always have a clear meaning.

---

# Verification Badges

Different badges represent different responsibilities.

Examples include:

## 🔴 Kizunia Owner

Assigned to the owner of the Kizunia platform.

Represents the highest level of authority.

---

## 🟣 Kizunia Administrator

Assigned to trusted administrators responsible for operating the platform.

---

## 🟢 Verified Organizer

Assigned to organizers or official representatives responsible for maintaining a hackathon.

---

## 🔵 Verified Project Owner

Indicates that ownership of a project has been verified by Kizunia.

---

## 🟡 Verified Team

Assigned to teams whose identity has been verified.

---

Additional verification badges may be introduced as the platform evolves.

Each badge should communicate responsibility rather than status.

---

# Featured Content

Administrators may feature high-quality content.

Examples include:

* Featured Projects
* Featured Teams
* Featured Hackathons
* Featured Builders

Featured content should highlight exceptional work while remaining transparent and fair.

---

# Reports

Users may report:

* Spam
* Incorrect information
* Broken links
* Inappropriate content
* Duplicate entries

Reports enter a moderation queue for review.

---

# Moderation

Moderation should focus on preserving quality rather than restricting participation.

Whenever possible:

* explain moderation decisions,
* maintain transparency,
* avoid unnecessary removals.

The goal is to build trust between the platform and its community.

---

# Audit History

Administrative actions should be recorded.

Examples include:

* Verification granted
* Verification revoked
* Hackathon approved
* Suggestion accepted
* Suggestion rejected
* Featured content updated

Maintaining an audit history improves transparency and accountability.

---

# Future Expansion

Future governance capabilities may include:

* Multi-stage review workflows
* Community moderators
* Reputation systems
* Trusted reviewer program
* Automated spam detection
* Governance analytics

These features should extend the existing governance model rather than replace it.

---

# Guiding Principle

Kizunia succeeds when its community can actively improve the platform while users continue to trust the accuracy of its information.

The role of governance is not to control the community.

Its role is to enable collaboration while protecting the quality, authenticity, and reliability of the platform.
