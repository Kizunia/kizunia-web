# Hackathons

## Purpose

The Hackathons module exists to help builders discover relevant hackathons, stay informed about upcoming opportunities, and connect with teams before registrations close.

Rather than functioning as a registration platform, Kizunia Web acts as a curated discovery platform that helps students find the right opportunities while connecting them with the community around those events.

Hackathons are one of the primary entry points into the Kizunia ecosystem.

---

# Philosophy

Finding hackathons should not depend on scattered WhatsApp groups, Discord servers, LinkedIn posts, Instagram stories, or word of mouth.

Kizunia Web aims to become a centralized place where students can reliably discover hackathons while still allowing the community to help keep information accurate and up to date.

The platform is community-driven but curated.

---

# Responsibilities

The Hackathons module is responsible for:

* Discovery
* Information
* Categorization
* Search
* Filtering
* Community contributions
* Verification
* Notifications

The module is **not** responsible for registrations, submissions, judging, or hosting hackathons.

Those responsibilities belong to the organizers.

---

# Hackathon Information

Each hackathon should contain comprehensive information.

This may include:

* Name
* Logo
* Banner
* Short Description
* Detailed Description
* Organizer
* Registration Link
* Official Website
* Registration Deadline
* Event Dates
* Mode (Online /Offline / Hybrid)
* Location
* Eligibility
* Prize Pool
* Categories
* Technologies
* Sponsors
* Resources
* Frequently Asked Questions

The goal is to provide everything a participant needs before deciding to register.

---

# Discovery

Users should be able to discover hackathons through:

* Search
* Categories
* Technologies
* Location
* Online / Offline
* Registration Status
* Eligibility
* Date
* Prize Pool
* Organizer

Finding relevant opportunities should require minimal effort.

---

# Categories

Hackathons may belong to one or more categories.

Examples include:

* AI
* Web
* Mobile
* Blockchain
* IoT
* Cybersecurity
* Robotics
* Healthcare
* FinTech
* Open Innovation

Additional categories may be introduced as the ecosystem grows.

---

# Bookmarking

Users may bookmark hackathons they are interested in.

Bookmarked hackathons become part of the user's notification preferences and allow quick access from their profile.

---

# Personalized Notifications

Notifications are one of the core features of Kizunia Web.

Users should receive notifications only for hackathons that match their interests.

Matching may consider:

* Preferred technologies
* Categories
* Location
* Online / Offline preference
* Student eligibility
* Registration deadlines

The goal is relevance rather than quantity.

---

# Community Contributions

Kizunia Web encourages community participation.

Any registered user may suggest a new hackathon.

Suggested hackathons enter a review queue where they are verified before becoming publicly visible.

This allows the platform to grow without relying solely on administrators while maintaining high-quality information.

---

# Suggesting a Hackathon

Users may submit information such as:

* Name
* Organizer
* Website
* Registration Link
* Description
* Dates
* Location
* Images
* Supporting Resources

The submitted information is reviewed before publication.

---

# Suggesting Edits

Community members may also suggest improvements to existing hackathons.

Examples include:

* Updated registration links
* Deadline changes
* Venue updates
* Sponsor changes
* Prize pool corrections
* Typographical fixes

Suggestions do not immediately modify published information.

They are reviewed before being accepted.

---

# Ownership

Every hackathon page has one or more owners.

Owners have complete administrative control over the hackathon page.

Owners may:

* Edit information
* Delete the page
* Archive the page
* Manage maintainers
* Approve changes

For most hackathons, ownership belongs to Kizunia administrators.

---

# Maintainers

Owners may assign maintainers.

Maintainers are trusted users who help keep hackathon information accurate.

Examples include:

* Organizers
* Club Core Members
* Event Coordinators
* Trusted Volunteers

Maintainers may:

* Update hackathon information
* Manage resources
* Edit documentation
* Update media
* Correct information

Maintainers cannot transfer ownership or permanently delete the hackathon page.

---

# Verification

Verification establishes trust.

Unlike traditional social media, verification represents authenticity and responsibility.

Examples include:

* Verified Organizer
* Verified Club Member
* Official Event Maintainer

Verification helps users identify who is responsible for maintaining information about a particular hackathon.

---

# Relationships

A hackathon may relate to:

* Multiple teams
* Multiple projects
* Multiple organizers
* Multiple maintainers

Projects and teams continue to exist independently after the hackathon concludes.

The hackathon acts as a relationship rather than an owner.

---

# Visibility

Hackathons are generally public.

Future visibility options may include:

* Public
* Unlisted
* Archived

Archived hackathons remain accessible for historical reference.

---

# Future Expansion

Potential future capabilities include:

* Registration tracking
* Timeline
* Winners
* Past editions
* Organizer profiles
* Community reviews
* Statistics
* Related hackathons

These features are intentionally outside the current MVP.

---

# Guiding Principle

Hackathons should be easy to discover, easy to understand, and easy to trust.

Kizunia Web should empower the community to contribute information while ensuring that published content remains accurate through verification, trusted maintainers, and thoughtful moderation.

The platform should become the first place students visit when searching for their next hackathon.
