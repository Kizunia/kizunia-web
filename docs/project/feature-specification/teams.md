# Teams

## Purpose

The Teams module enables builders to collaborate on projects.

While Kizunia Web allows users to create projects individually, many engineering projects are built by groups of people with different skills and responsibilities.

The Teams module exists to make finding, forming, and managing those groups significantly easier.

Teams are one of the primary reasons Kizunia Web exists.

---

# Philosophy

Finding the right teammates should not depend on WhatsApp messages, Discord servers, Instagram stories, or personal contacts.

Kizunia Web provides a structured platform where builders can discover each other based on skills, interests, experience, and shared goals.

Teams should be easy to create, easy to discover, and easy to manage.

---

# Responsibilities

The Teams module is responsible for:

* Team identity
* Team recruitment
* Membership management
* Collaboration
* Project ownership
* Hackathon participation

It is not responsible for messaging, task management, or source control.

Those concerns belong to other platforms or future modules.

---

# Team Profile

Every team has its own profile.

A team profile represents the group rather than an individual.

A team profile may include:

* Team Name
* Team Logo
* Banner
* Short Description
* About
* Skills Required
* Current Status
* Recruitment Status
* Contact Information
* Associated Projects
* Associated Hackathons

---

# Team Creation

Any registered user may create a team.

The creator automatically becomes the first owner of the team.

Additional owners may be added later.

---

# Recruitment

One of the primary goals of the Teams module is recruitment.

Teams should be able to recruit builders with specific skills.

Examples include:

* Frontend Developer
* Backend Developer
* Mobile Developer
* AI Engineer
* UI/UX Designer
* DevOps Engineer
* Product Designer

Recruitment should remain open until the team decides to close it.

---

# Joining a Team

Users may discover and request to join teams.

A request does not automatically grant membership.

Team owners or maintainers decide whether to accept or reject requests.

Future versions may support invitation-only teams.

---

# Membership

A team may contain multiple members.

Members may have different responsibilities within the team.

Membership should not imply ownership.

Similarly, ownership should not imply project contribution.

Each relationship is intentionally managed independently.

---

# Ownership

Every team has one or more owners.

Owners have complete control over the team.

Owners may:

* Edit team information
* Manage members
* Promote maintainers
* Remove members
* Archive the team
* Delete the team
* Transfer ownership

---

# Maintainers

Maintainers help manage the team.

They may:

* Edit team information
* Manage recruitment
* Accept applications
* Invite members
* Update team details

Maintainers cannot transfer ownership or permanently delete the team.

---

# Members

Members belong to the team.

Being a member does not automatically grant administrative privileges.

Members may:

* Participate in projects
* Participate in hackathons
* Represent the team

Additional permissions may be granted by the owners if required.

---

# Recruitment Status

Teams may define their recruitment status.

Examples include:

* Recruiting
* Invite Only
* Full
* Closed

This helps users quickly understand whether a team is accepting new members.

---

# Skills

Teams may specify desired skills.

Examples include:

* React
* Flutter
* Python
* Java
* AI/ML
* UI/UX
* DevOps

Skills improve discoverability and help builders find teams that match their expertise.

---

# Projects

Teams may own multiple projects.

Projects may also exist without teams.

Likewise, teams may exist before creating their first project.

Relationships remain intentionally flexible.

---

# Hackathons

Teams may participate in multiple hackathons.

Participation in one hackathon should not require creating a new team.

Long-term teams should be encouraged.

---

# Discoverability

Users should be able to discover teams through:

* Skills
* Technologies
* Categories
* Recruitment Status
* Hackathons
* Projects

The goal is to connect builders rather than simply listing teams.

---

# Visibility

Teams may support different visibility levels.

Examples include:

* Public
* Unlisted
* Private

Private teams may still participate in projects while remaining hidden from public searches.

---

# Verification

Teams may receive verification from Kizunia.

Verification confirms authenticity rather than popularity.

Examples include:

* Verified Team
* Official Team

Verification is managed through the platform administration system.

---

# Future Expansion

Future versions of the Teams module may include:

* Team roles
* Team portfolios
* Team achievements
* Team statistics
* Team invitations
* Team recommendations
* Team history

These additions should extend the existing model without fundamentally changing it.

---

# Guiding Principle

Teams exist to make collaboration easier.

The module should prioritize helping builders find the right people, build meaningful projects together, and continue collaborating beyond a single hackathon.

A team should be viewed as a long-term collaborative unit rather than a temporary group formed only for a competition.
