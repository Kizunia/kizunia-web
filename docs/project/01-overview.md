# Kizunia Web Overview

## Introduction

Kizunia Web is the official web platform developed and maintained under the **Kizunia** GitHub organization.

While **Kizunia** represents the community, organization, and collaborative ecosystem, **Kizunia Web** is the primary product that powers that ecosystem.

It provides a centralized platform where student developers can discover hackathons, connect with teammates, showcase their projects, and build a public engineering portfolio through real collaboration.

---

# About Kizunia

Kizunia is an organization dedicated to encouraging collaboration, innovation, and engineering culture among students.

Its goals include:

* Bringing student developers together.
* Encouraging collaboration across colleges and communities.
* Preserving hackathon and student projects.
* Promoting open-source contributions.
* Building software that helps students learn, collaborate, and grow.

---

# Why Kizunia Web Exists

Every year, thousands of students participate in hackathons.

Before the event they struggle to:

* Discover relevant hackathons.
* Find teammates with complementary skills.
* Know whether they are eligible for a competition.

During the event they struggle to:

* Build effective teams.
* Coordinate contributors.
* Showcase progress.

After the event, most projects disappear.

GitHub repositories become inactive.

Presentation links expire.

Team members move on.

Months of engineering effort are often forgotten.

At the same time, students manually maintain portfolios that quickly become outdated because they require constant effort to update.

Kizunia Web aims to solve these problems by creating a platform that naturally connects people, teams, projects, and hackathons.

---

# Mission

To help student builders discover opportunities, form meaningful teams, create impactful projects, and preserve their work through a collaborative platform.

---

# Vision

To become the platform students think of first when they want to:

* Discover hackathons.
* Build a team.
* Collaborate on projects.
* Showcase their engineering journey.

Rather than existing as another portfolio website or hackathon listing platform, Kizunia Web aims to become a home for student builders.

---

# Product Pillars

The product is built around five primary pillars.

## Discover

Students should be able to easily discover hackathons relevant to their interests, skills, location, and preferences.

Finding opportunities should be effortless rather than relying on scattered social media posts or college groups.

---

## Connect

Finding the right teammates should be a first-class experience.

Students should be able to create teams, join existing teams, and collaborate with developers who complement their skills.

---

## Build

Projects are the heart of Kizunia Web.

The platform should allow projects to represent real collaboration by supporting multiple contributors, optional teams, optional hackathons, repositories, demos, and supporting resources.

---

## Showcase

A student's work should speak for itself.

Instead of manually maintaining a portfolio, Kizunia Web generates one using the user's activity across projects, hackathons, and teams.

---

## Preserve

Hackathons should not mark the end of a project.

Projects should remain accessible, discoverable, and maintainable after the competition ends.

This principle reflects one of the core goals of the Kizunia organization: preserving meaningful student work.

---

# Core Platform Components

The current version of Kizunia Web focuses on five core components.

* Users
* Hackathons
* Teams
* Projects
* Portfolios

These components work together while remaining loosely coupled so that future features can be introduced without redesigning the platform.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every major decision within Kizunia Web follows a few important ideas.

### Real Problems First

The platform exists to solve genuine problems experienced by students, beginning with hackathon discovery and team formation.

---

### Flexible Relationships

Relationships should be flexible whenever possible.

For example:

* A project may belong to a hackathon.
* A project may belong to a team.
* A project may be completely independent.

The platform should support real-world workflows instead of forcing a single structure.

---

### Activity Over Manual Data

Information should be generated from user activity whenever possible.

Projects, hackathons, and contributions should naturally build a user's portfolio instead of requiring repetitive manual updates.

---

### Grow Incrementally

Kizunia Web is intentionally being developed in phases.

Only features that provide meaningful value today are included in the MVP.

Future capabilities will build upon the existing foundation rather than replacing it.

---

# Current Scope

The first version of Kizunia Web focuses on solving four primary problems:

* Helping students discover hackathons.
* Helping students form teams.
* Providing a home for collaborative projects.
* Building meaningful developer portfolios.

Everything else is considered future work until these core experiences are polished.

---

# Relationship with the Kizunia Organization

Kizunia Web is one of the projects maintained by the Kizunia organization.

The organization may host additional open-source repositories, tools, libraries, templates, or future products.

Although closely related, the Kizunia organization and Kizunia Web should be viewed as separate concepts:

* **Kizunia** is the organization and community.
* **Kizunia Web** is the software platform developed by that organization.

This distinction allows the organization to grow beyond a single project while keeping Kizunia Web focused on its mission.
